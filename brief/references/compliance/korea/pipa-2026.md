# PIPA 2026 Amendment — Primer

> Not legal advice. Refer to qualified Korean counsel before acting on findings.

## Scope

The Personal Information Protection Act (개인정보 보호법, PIPA) 2026 amendment applies to all
personal-information controllers operating in Korea or processing data of Korean residents. The
amendment was promulgated 2026-03-10 and takes effect 2026-09-11. Controllers processing data of
5+ million data subjects trigger additional obligations. The amendment expanded the territorial
reach of PIPA enforcement beyond prior scope.

## Key Articles / Clauses (high-level)

- **Art. 28-8 (supervisory responsibility)** — The CEO / 대표이사 is designated as the ultimate
  responsible person for personal-information protection supervision within the organization.
- **Art. 64-2 (penalty ceiling)** — Administrative fine ceiling raised to 10% of total turnover
  (previously capped lower).
- **Art. 34 (breach notification)** — 2026 amendment introduces a probabilistic
  incident-notification trigger (not solely time-based).
- **Art. 31 (CPO independence)** — The 2026 amendment mandates CPO (Chief Privacy Officer)
  operational independence from the security team.

## Common Gotchas

- **Single blanket consent checkbox EXPLICITLY FORBIDDEN.** Each data-processing category requires
  separate specific consent. This is the #1 cause of public enforcement actions in 2025.
- **"Dark patterns" monitored by PIPC.** Cookie banners, subscription cancel flows, and consent
  withdrawal UX are enforcement-relevant.
- **Cross-border transfer adequacy.** PIPC maintains a list of safe-harbor jurisdictions; transfers
  to non-adequate jurisdictions require explicit consent AND data-processing addendums (DPA).
- **Sensitive data scope.** Location, biometric, and children's data carry additional restrictions
  beyond the baseline consent discipline.

## Penalties + CEO Liability

- **Administrative fines:** up to 10% of total turnover. This is the most-cited change in the 2026
  amendment and raises the material-risk calculus significantly for mid-market and enterprise
  controllers.
- **CEO / 대표이사 personal supervisory liability.** The 2026 amendment clarifies that the CEO is
  personally responsible for supervision; criminal exposure is possible under certain breach
  scenarios. This is the single highest-severity signal for planning artifacts: a B2B planner
  targeting Korean enterprise customers should surface this early in any PRD or compliance review.
- **ISMS-P certification becomes mandatory 2027-07-01** for designated large-scale controllers
  (see `isms-p.md` for detail).
